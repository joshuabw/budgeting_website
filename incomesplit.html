Your REAL income


Uncle Sam will take first dibs on your paycheck. This will account for federal taxes, Social Security and Medicare combined. Along with state taxes and workplace benefits. Workplace benefits like medical and dental insurance or a retirement savings plan. Whether or not you want to calculate your budget before or after those expenses is up to you.


You do You.

The budget breakdown above is only a estimator. Every person will have different budgets according to their lifestyle i.e. single vs. married, kids vs. no kids. So it is important to readjust your budget to exactly fit your needs.
If you live with roommates, you rent may be below 30% and if you have lots of credit card and student loan debt, you may need to increase your debt repayment. Know what works for you.




Getting an exact measure of how much you are spending


Utilities


Where you live may increase or decrease your utility bill. Someone in Nevada may have a higher electricity bill than someone living in New York. Air conditioning, and heat are factors you want to consider when creating your budget.

Internet, Cable & Phone


Thinking about using a family cell phone plan as opposed to a single plan will be cheaper. Or thinking about changing service providers will also provide helpful.
Also, cutting cable and buying a chromecast or Roku will also save you in the long run.
