Why do you need a FICO score?



  • Credit score is needed when you want to borrow money
  • Ex: you want to apply for a credit card, car loan or a mortgage for a house

  • Lenders want to know what risk they would take by loaning you money

  • Your credit score will influence the lenders or banks decision to lend you money, and the terms that they may offer like the interest rate, or the amount of money they want to lend.

  • FICO scores range from 350 to 850 score range
  • General rule of : the higher the score the lower the risk.

How is your FICO score calculated?

  • Score is calculated using five components: Payment history, Amount of debts, Length of credit history, New Credit, Mixed Credit
  1. Payment history: 35%
    -ability to pay your credit accounts on time
  2. Amount of debt: 30%
    -Amount of money you owe compared to your credit limit
    -You want to owe a smaller amount of money compared to how much money you can owe
  3. Length of credit history: 15%
    -longer credit histories will usually increase your score
  4. New Credit: 10%
    -opening several credit account in a short period of time indicates you are a greater risk for lenders
  5. Credit Mix: 10%
    -There are many different types of credit you can have, credit cards, retail accounts, finance company accounts and mortgages




Not happy with your FICO score?

No problem, here are some ways to increase your FICO score

  • Watch how much revolving credit you have vs. how much you're actually using. The smaller percentage you are actually using, the better for your credit rating.
  • Eliminate credit card balances. Small balances you have on a number of credit cards . Depends on how many cards have balances. ...... BLALAAL
  • Good Debt vs. Bad Debt. Debt that you have handled well and paid as agreed is good for your credit. Don't clost old accounts that you've had a solid repayment record.
  • Choose wisely! Every time you apply for credit, it can cause a small dip in your credit score that lasts a year.
  • Last but not least, pay your bills on time.....
